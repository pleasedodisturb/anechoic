## Coming On Board

### The Trial Process

When you make it past the interview stage, we'll do a **paid project together on contract**, typically lasting between two to six weeks depending on how much time you can spend, to see how we work together.

### Support Rotation

**When you join full-time, you'll do customer support for WordPress.com for your first two weeks** and spend a week in support annually, for evermore, regardless of your position.

> We believe an early and ongoing connection with the people who use our products is irreplaceable.

## Meeting Up

> "Despite being a fully distributed company, I believe it's still important to meet face-to-face — just not every day, in the same office." — Matt Mullenweg, Founder

### Company Gatherings

Prior to the pandemic, we would get the whole company together once a year for seven days so Automatticians could create bonds that influence them all year long.

Since the pandemic, we focus on **smaller groups and division meetups** that accomplish bonding and coworking on a more manageable scale.

### Team Meetups

Individual teams meet for **five to seven days** to brainstorm team-level strategy and bond in locales ranging from Boulder to Buenos Aires, Las Vegas to Lisbon, Montréal to Mexico City, and Vienna to Vietnam.

**If you join, expect to travel three to four weeks per year.**

### Day-to-Day Social Communication

As a distributed company, we also know how to connect with each other from afar, and consider **day-to-day social communication at least as important as breaking bread in person**.

## The Hiring Process

### 1. Application
You submit a formal application, giving us a sense of your communication style. If your application advances, we'll invite you to complete a criteria assessment.

### 2. Interview
One of the first steps is a Slack or Zoom interview. While some roles start with a written interview on Slack, others require Zoom interviews.

### 3. Trial
A **paid trial** in the application process — a short project or set of tasks assessed by our hiring teams. You'll tackle a specific, real problem.

### 4. Final Interview
Once you successfully complete your trial, many teams require a final interview — typically conducted by an executive leader.

### 5. The Offer
Our HR folks will share feedback from the hiring process and tender a formal offer to join Automattic.
