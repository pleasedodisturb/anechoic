# The Complete Guide to Asynchronous and Non-Linear Working

*Source: GitLab Handbook - https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/company/culture/all-remote/non-linear-workday/*

On this page, we're detailing what life can look and feel like when embracing a non-linear workday, paired with suggestions on catalyzing your imagination to consider possibilities that simply are not possible in a colocated, synchronous workplace.

---

## What is a Non-Linear Workday Routine?

A non-linear workday means that you can move between work and non-work time on an asynchronous schedule — **without needing to account for PTO (paid-time-off)**.

### Example

A remote worker may:
- Start their day at 6:00 AM and work till 9:00 AM local time
- Spend time with family (not checking work email)
- Resume working at 3:00 PM local time
- Continue to work until 8:00 PM local time
- Tomorrow may be a totally different schedule depending on work/life circumstances

---

## Breaking Preconceived Notions About Routine

A common recommendation for those new to remote work is to find a routine early on, and stick to it. While this may be sound advice for *some*, it ignores the reality that **remote enables a complete deconstruction of the perceived need for routine**.

### Why Routine Exists

**Routine is a common suggestion not necessarily because it is *good*, but because it is *tradition*.**

- Routine is mandated in a colocated environment, where team members are required to commute and work between fixed hours
- We have been conditioned to believe that routine keeps us disciplined, when in reality, routine simply makes it easier for colocated companies to keep workers in line
- Many people adhere to routines simply because they know no other way

### What Enables Breaking Free

Remote allows another option, thanks to:
- The tremendous benefits of asynchronous workflows
- Handbook-first documentation
- Company-wide transparency

## A Real Non-Linear Workday Example

This is an actual example from a GitLab team member:

**6:00 AM** - Wake up in Montana
**6:30 AM** - Start workday after 30-second commute from one room to another
**8:30 AM** - End first work block. Change Slack status to a skier emoji, noting to colleagues he'll be out through 4:30 PM local time
**10:00 AM** - Family is skiing. Almost no one on the mountain (most people are in offices). Lift lines nonexistent. Lift tickets heavily discounted.
**3:00 PM** - Ski back to the car, refreshed
**3:30 PM** - Back home (left before rush hour)
**4:30 PM** - Log back on to work, share photos of skiing day
**5:00 PM** - It's fairly dark outside. Darren has maximized his daylight hours, and time-shifted his working day to primarily occur during darkness
**10:30 PM** - End workday

### Key Insights

> We should pause at this point and recognize that time is still relative. When Darren resumes his workday at 4:30 PM, he has six more hours to contribute if working a standard eight-hour day. It is important to not get caught up in local times.
> 
> 4:30 PM may sound like an absurd time to resume working, but that's morning, afternoon, and night for various other members on his team.

## Examples of Non-Linear Workdays

You can experience non-linear workdays on a spectrum. Practical, everyday ways to break up your day:

- **Parents and caregivers** — Take time during morning and afternoon to take children to school
- **Runners** — Schedule training sessions around meetings
- **Fitness enthusiasts** — Schedule midday fitness breaks (ideal for those not keen on exercising first thing in the morning or after their workday)
- **Parents of toddlers** — Schedule meeting blocks based on evolving nap schedule
- **Volunteers** — Create availability to volunteer during daylight hours at a food bank, library, community center, etc.
- **Life admin** — Handle everyday tasks (oil change, meeting with contractors, etc.) during non-peak hours

## Benefits of a Non-Linear Workday

### Flexibility
A non-linear workday empowers team members to live and work both when and where they're most productive.

### Autonomy
Team members are given agency to move projects forward on a schedule that suits them. Because GitLab measures impact, not activity, people are free to achieve results when it best suits them.

### Efficiency
Remote decouples routine from responsibility. Employees who enjoy being a manager of one thrive in a remote setting, and often exhibit *more* discipline to work well when no one is looking.

---

## Challenges of a Non-Linear Workday

### Juggling Time Zones
Depending on where you and your teammates are primarily based, it may be a struggle to communicate well with others in a different time zone.

### Staying Organized
One challenging aspect is delineating where one working session ends and another begins. There is often no reason or excuse to stop or shift tasks other than "the clock tells me it's time to stop."

### Distractions
For novice remote workers, it's important to think about when and where you prefer to work on a daily basis. Optimize your physical and mental space for focus and design a space that prevents distractions.

---

## Common Distractions When Working Remotely

The most common distractions are:
- Ambient sounds
- Visual distractions
- Working in areas with high traffic

**Solution:** Attempt to dedicate both physical and mental space where only work occurs. This enables you to focus specifically on work and be sure to healthily disconnect when you exit the space.

## Best Ways to Stay Productive When Working Remotely

It can be tempting to default to old ways, whereas working a non-linear schedule requires doing things differently.

> **There's as much to unlearn as there is to learn** when it comes to thriving in a remote role.

### Pro Tips

1. **Learn communication preferences** — Adapt to the communication preferences of your co-workers
2. **Bolster your internet connection** — Have a backup plan
3. **Monitor your mental health** and emotional health
4. **Be intentional about informal communication** to foster co-working relationships

---

## Tips for Staying Organized When Working Remotely

- Invest time learning the company's technology & tools to maximize efficiency
- Develop a schedule that you can test and tweak
- Create strategic connections with co-workers
- Set up work-life boundaries

### Key Insight

**The non-linear workday decouples time from work and acts as a forcing function to embrace asynchronous workflows.**

Local times are only as important as your company relies on synchronicity to get things done. The more this bothers you, the further you need to distance your organization from synchronous defaults.

## Fostering Your Imagination

Ask yourself these questions:

- **What could your life be if no two days were ever the same?**
- **What do you long to do that you think a work routine is holding you back from?**
- **How would you integrate work into your life if you were given permission to scrap everything you thought you knew about the importance of routines?**

Answering the above will allow you to truly evaluate what elements of routine are beneficial to you, and which are holding you back.

### The Ultimate Life Cheat Code

> What could life look like if I never had to commute again? If I did not have to be in this particular city for work?
> 
> What you're seeing en masse (due to COVID-19) is people being thrust into remote, and they're trying to replicate virtually the in-office experience.
> 
> **I want people to give themselves permission to realize that remote is an entirely new universe.** You don't have the commute. You don't have the stigma and office expectations. You can potentially shift your day to work during your peak productivity hours, which opens up hours that have never been accessible to you before.
> 
> **It's the ultimate blank slate, the ultimate life cheat code.**
> 
> There tends to be remoter's guilt, where you feel like you need to over-produce as a remote employee to compensate for the removal of a commute. **The commute was never the employer's to begin with; that was always your time.**
> 
> You can do something with that. You can be innovative with it.

## What is Required to Enable Non-Linear Workdays?

The example detailed here would not have been possible without a few realities already in place:

### 1. Handbook-First Approach
The company must work handbook-first, such that all meaningful takeaways from conversations are documented in their proper place, around the clock.

### 2. Asynchronous Workflows
The company must embrace asynchronous workflows (including tools as well as processes) in a deliberate, intentional, and thorough way.

### 3. Non-Judgmental Culture
The company must support a non-judgmental culture, which measures team members on impact rather than activity. This enables people to enter and exit work as they so choose, with no fear of retribution.

### 4. Personal Dedication
A personal dedication to being a manager of one, able to focus on the right tasks while working from a foreign environment.

### 5. Realistic Expectations
An understanding that not every single day will look like this. Even masters of non-linear workdays recognize that some days are less amenable to midday excursions than others.

**Rather than being sour about that, embrace the thrill of it being possible at all**, and put effort into structuring your upcoming schedule in a way that allows for such days.

### Key Reminder

> As a manager of one, you have to take control over (and be accountable for) your schedule. Otherwise, other forces of the world and work will control it for you.

## What About Meetings?

**The obvious question:** "How do you leave work during a time when meetings are most likely to be scheduled?"

**The not-so-obvious answer:** Create a workplace culture where meetings are a last resort, and ensure that unavoidable meetings can be contributed to asynchronously.

### Key Principles

- Not every single day will present itself as a natural, meeting-free day
- The more intentional your company is about ruthlessly minimizing meetings, the more feasible non-linear workdays become
- Separate decision gathering from decision making
- Insist that all work begin where it eventually needs to end up (e.g., in an issue or merge request)

## Find What Works for You

While embracing non-linear workdays can be incredibly freeing, it's also important to recognize that **breaking daily routines might not be productive for everyone**.

Whether your schedule:
- Looks different each day
- Sticks to a strict routine
- Incorporates elements of both

**The key is knowing that you have the freedom and autonomy to decide what's best for you.**

## Set Clear Priorities: The 3 Tasks System

Non-linear workdays are focused on results. Building systems to focus on results helps avoid overwhelm and burnout.

### Summary

**The goal is to achieve 3 meaningful outcomes every day, week, month, and year.** Small outcomes add up to meaningful and long-term results.

### Why 3 Tasks?

- Productivity is about doing the right things at the right time
- Choosing 3 tasks forces prioritization
- Helps avoid context switching

### Key Benefits

1. Reduction in time spent on unimportant work
2. Improved personal efficiency and effectiveness
3. Increased sense of accomplishment
4. Increased results over time

### How to Implement

- Choose a recording system that works for you: notepad, sticky notes, reminders, issues, etc.
- Each day, choose what needs to get done today and decide what can be punted to future days/months
- When other things come up outside of your 3 tasks, write them down on a "next" or "future" card
- **Write them down so you can remove the pressure of having to remember these tasks**
- Adapt this system to meet your own needs
