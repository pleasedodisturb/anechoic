# Basecamp (37signals) Handbook - Comprehensive Summary

## Company Philosophy

### The Calm Company
- **Protect people's time** - Interruptions are the enemy of productivity
- **40-hour weeks** - Sustainable pace, no hero culture
- **Profitability over growth** - No external pressure from investors
- **Small team, big impact** - Do more with less
- **Writing over talking** - Best ideas win, not loudest voice

### Core Beliefs
- Work doesn't happen at work (offices are interruption factories)
- Real-time is rarely required
- Meetings are toxic (last resort, not first)
- Planning is guessing (embrace uncertainty)
- Ship and iterate (good enough today > perfect never)

## Communication Practices

### Daily Q&A (Automatic Check-ins)
- **"What did you work on today?"** - Posted automatically in Basecamp
- Answered **twice weekly** (not daily - reduces burden)
- Visible to whole company
- Replaces standup meetings entirely
- Creates natural accountability without micromanagement

### Weekly Check-ins
- **"What will you be working on this week?"**
- Posted Monday mornings
- Gives visibility into priorities
- Enables async coordination
- No meeting required

### Heartbeats
- **Monthly/cycle updates** from teams
- What was accomplished
- What's planned next
- Shared company-wide
- Celebrates wins publicly

### Kickoffs
- **Start of each 6-week cycle**
- Shape Up methodology
- Written pitch documents
- Async review before any meeting
- Clear scope and appetite

## The 6-Week Cycle (Shape Up)

### Structure
- **6 weeks on, 2 weeks off (cooldown)**
- No sprints, no story points
- Projects are "shaped" before committed
- Betting table decides what ships
- Teams have full autonomy during cycle

### Shaping
- Senior people shape work before teams see it
- Written pitches with problem, appetite, solution
- Rabbit holes identified upfront
- "No-gos" explicitly called out
- Visual sketches (fat marker, not wireframes)

### Betting
- Leadership "bets" on shaped work
- No backlog - if not bet, it's dropped
- Clear commitment for 6 weeks
- No scope creep allowed
- Ship or kill at end of cycle

### Cooldown
- 2 weeks between cycles
- Fix bugs, explore ideas
- No scheduled work
- Recovery and exploration time
- Prevents burnout

## Async Defaults

### When to Write vs. Meet
| Write (Async) | Meet (Sync) |
|---------------|-------------|
| Status updates | Sensitive personnel |
| Decisions (most) | Heated debates |
| Feedback | Relationship building |
| Planning | Urgent incidents |
| Questions | Nothing else works |

### Writing Standards
- **Long-form over chat** - Basecamp posts, not Slack
- **Complete thoughts** - Don't fragment across messages
- **Give time to respond** - 24h minimum for non-urgent
- **Thread everything** - Keep conversations organized
- **Summarize at end** - Capture decisions inline

### The 15-Minute Rule
- Don't interrupt someone unless you've tried to solve it yourself for 15 minutes
- Most questions answer themselves
- Teaches self-sufficiency
- Respects deep work time

## Work Boundaries

### No Surveillance
- No time tracking software
- No always-on video
- No "green dot" pressure
- Trust people to do their work
- Judge by output, not presence

### Calendar Respect
- **Maker's schedule** protected (long blocks for deep work)
- **Meeting-free days** encouraged
- **Default no** to meetings
- **Async first** always

### After Hours
- **Shutdown ritual** - Clear end to workday
- **No expectation of after-hours response**
- **Weekend work discouraged**
- **Vacation is vacation** - Truly offline

---

## Decision Making

### Disagree and Commit
- Debate thoroughly in writing
- Once decided, everyone commits
- No relitigating
- Move forward together

### Who Decides
- People closest to the work
- Not committees
- Clear ownership
- Fast decisions

### Documentation
- Decisions written in Basecamp
- Context preserved
- Searchable forever
- New hires can understand history

## Hiring & Onboarding

### Remote-First Hiring
- Global candidates welcome
- Work samples over interviews
- Paid trial projects
- Values fit critical
- Writing ability essential

### Onboarding
- **Gradual immersion** - Don't drink from firehose
- **Buddy assigned** - Go-to person for questions
- **Read the handbook** - Self-service learning
- **Ship something early** - Quick wins build confidence

## Key Takeaways for TPMs

1. **Replace standups with async check-ins** - "What did you work on?" posted twice weekly
2. **Shape before committing** - Written pitches with clear appetite
3. **Protect maker time** - Default to no meetings
4. **Write long-form** - Complete thoughts, not chat fragments
5. **6-week cycles** - Sustainable rhythm with built-in breaks
6. **Shutdown ritual** - Model healthy boundaries
