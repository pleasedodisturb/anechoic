# Help Scout Handbook - Comprehensive Summary

**Source:** [helpscout.com](https://www.helpscout.com) & public articles  
**Company:** Help Scout (~80-100 employees, fully remote)  
**Key Innovation:** Paid project hiring, Buddy system, FIKA rituals  
**Format:** Summarized into core bulletpoints per section

---

## Company Philosophy

### Remote-First Values
- **Trust as foundation** - Assume good intent
- **Work-life harmony** - Not just balance
- **Quality over speed** - Do it right
- **Transparency** - Share early and often
- **Continuous improvement** - Always learning

### "Guide to the Galaxy"
- Internal wiki/handbook (hosted on Slab)
- Single source of truth
- Living document, always updated
- Onboarding starting point
- Cultural values documented

---

## Hiring Practices

### Paid Project Assessment
- **All candidates paid for assessment work**
- Real work, not hypotheticals
- Respects candidates' time
- Better signal than interviews
- Demonstrates company values

### Hiring Process
1. Application review
2. Initial async questions (written)
3. Paid project (1-2 weeks, part-time)
4. Team interviews (limited, focused)
5. Reference checks
6. Offer

### What They Look For
- Strong written communication
- Self-direction and initiative
- Empathy and customer focus
- Remote work capability
- Cultural alignment

### Hiring Team Structure
- Cross-functional hiring committee
- Clear rubrics for evaluation
- Multiple perspectives
- Bias reduction focus
- Decision by consensus

## Onboarding

### The Buddy System
- **Every new hire gets a buddy**
- Not their manager
- Go-to for questions
- Cultural guide
- Meets regularly first 90 days

### Buddy Responsibilities
- Daily check-ins first week
- Weekly check-ins first month
- Bi-weekly after that
- Answer "dumb questions"
- Introduce to team members

### 30/60/90 Day Plan
| Period | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| Day 1-30 | Learn systems, meet team, first contributions |
| Day 31-60 | Independent work, deeper relationships |
| Day 61-90 | Full productivity, identify improvements |

### Onboarding Documentation
- Comprehensive guides for every role
- Video walkthroughs of tools
- FAQs for common questions
- Access to full company history
- No assumptions about knowledge

## Communication Practices

### FIKA Rituals
- **Borrowed from Swedish coffee break culture**
- Random pairing for virtual coffee
- 15-30 minute social calls
- Non-work conversations
- Builds relationships across teams

### Communication Channels
| Channel | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Slack | Quick questions, social |
| Slab | Documentation, wiki |
| Email | External, formal |
| Zoom | Scheduled sync |
| Basecamp | Project work |

### Async Norms
- Default to async
- Write complete thoughts
- Thread conversations
- Set clear expectations
- Respond within 24h (non-urgent)

## Meeting Philosophy

### Meeting Types
- **1:1s** - Weekly with manager
- **Team syncs** - Weekly or bi-weekly
- **All-hands** - Monthly, recorded
- **FIKA** - Random social pairing
- **Retreats** - Annual in-person

### Meeting Rules
- Agenda required
- Notes taken and shared
- Recording for async access
- Camera optional
- End with action items

---

## Team Retreats

### Annual Gathering
- Whole company meets in person
- One week, rotating locations
- Mix of work and social
- Builds trust for remote work
- Significant investment

### Retreat Structure
- Strategic planning sessions
- Team bonding activities
- Hackathon/innovation time
- Social events
- No laptops at dinner

### Why Retreats Matter
- Human connection fuels async work
- Harder conversations easier in person
- Culture reinforced
- Alignment achieved
- Memories created

## Performance & Growth

### Feedback Culture
- Regular, not annual
- Specific and actionable
- Both directions (up and down)
- Written and verbal
- Growth-focused

### Career Development
- Individual development plans
- Learning budget
- Conference attendance
- Internal mobility
- Clear progression paths

### Performance Reviews
- Quarterly check-ins
- Self-assessment component
- Peer feedback included
- Goals and outcomes focus
- No stack ranking

## Work-Life Practices

### Flexible Work
- No set hours (within reason)
- Results-based evaluation
- Timezone consideration
- Family-friendly policies
- Mental health days

### Time Off
- Generous PTO
- Company-wide holidays
- Encouraged vacation use
- Real disconnection expected
- Coverage planned

### Burnout Prevention
- Sustainable pace
- Workload monitoring
- Check-ins on wellbeing
- Manager responsibility
- Lead by example

## Documentation Culture

### Slab as Wiki
- All policies documented
- Searchable and organized
- Anyone can contribute
- Regular reviews and updates
- Source of truth

### Documentation Standards
- Write for newcomers
- Include context and why
- Link related documents
- Date significant changes
- Review quarterly

## Key Takeaways for TPMs

2. **Buddy system** - Dedicated guide for every new hire

4. **Annual retreats** - In-person time enables better remote work

6. **90-day structure** - Clear milestones for new hire success
